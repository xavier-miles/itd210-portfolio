Local service since 2024

In January 2024, I moved from working in retail to pursuing further personal growth, and decided to begin doing animal care as a side hustle. If you live in the Lake, there's a good chance you've seen me walking a dog or two in the last couple of years. While I primarily take care of dogs and cats, I've also helped care for a snake. I've cared for all manner of dogs, large and small, and can handle walking more than one dog at the same time.

My approach to animal care

A happy caretaker is a happy animal, so I try to treat all my bookings with joy and positivity. I always try to treat each animal with the care that I would a pet of my own. When I'm provided instructions for a pet's needs, I dutifully follow them to make sure that the animal (and home) is safe, happy, and healthy.

Pet owner for 20 years

I was born and raised in Lake Barcroft. I've lived here my entire life, and I love the people and the environment. From the Lake itself, to the rolling hills, to its position nicely between several larger cities - and of course its proximity to D.C. - I've always felt at home.
In elementary school, we had a few bearded dragon lizards. I also had a couple betta fish of my own, as I learned how to take care of other living creatures. In 2012, we adopted my first dog, Sophie. I'd been wanting a dog for a few years, and my parents finally decided that I was mature enough to get one. We went to a few animal shelters, but didn't find the right dog for me. At one point, there was an expo of dogs up for adoption at a nearby store, and so we decided to check it out. As soon as I saw her, I knew she was the one I wanted, so we took her home. Over the 12 years we had Sophie, we did road trips together, went camping, explored parks and so much more.

Academic pursuits

In late 2024, I did a short 'career lab' with Maxx Potential which exposed me to a bit of the fields of cybersecurity, website development, AI training, and other IT sectors. I found myself drawn to website development, and in 2024, I did a short internship with Maxx where I worked with a small team on building a website. Website development struck me as an interesting way to build something both functional and with some creativity in it, and I decided to pursue it as a career. I enrolled in NVCC's website development certificate program, and started working on my development and design skills.
